Phase 1: Diplomatic Failure and Military Buildup (Feb 2025 – June 2025) 
February – March 2025: U.S. intelligence assesses that Iran is not yet building a nuclear weapon.
April 2025: President Trump initiates a two-month ultimatum for Iran to secure a new nuclear deal.
April 30, 2025: Iran executes a man accused of working for the Mossad.
May 31, 2025: The IAEA reports Iran has enough 60% enriched uranium for nine nuclear weapons.
June 12, 2025: The IAEA board passes a resolution declaring Iran non-compliant with nuclear obligations. 

Phase 2: The "Twelve-Day War" (June 13 – 24, 2025) 
June 13: Israel launches Operation Red Wedding, a surprise preemptive strike on 100 targets, including the Natanz Nuclear Facility. 30 Iranian generals and nine nuclear scientists are killed.
June 14–21: Iran retaliates with over 550 ballistic missiles and 1,000 drones, striking Tel Aviv, Haifa, and a hospital in Beersheba. Israel expands strikes to Iran's energy infrastructure.
June 22: The U.S. joins the conflict, bombing three nuclear sites in Natanz, Fordow, and Isfahan.
June 24: A U.S.-brokered ceasefire takes effect. Casualties include over 600 Iranians and 28 Israelis. 

Phase 3: Interim Tensions and Civil Unrest (July 2025 – January 2026) 
July 2, 2025: Iran formally halts cooperation with the IAEA, barring inspectors.
August 12, 2025: Iranian authorities arrest 21,000 people suspected of dissent following the June war.
Late 2025: Israel achieves operational status for the Iron Beam laser-based interception system.
December 2025 – January 2026: Massive anti-government protests break out in Iran. The government imposes a nationwide internet blackout for over two weeks. 

Phase 4: The 2026 Iran War (February 28, 2026 – Present) 
Late January 2026: Several Gulf states reportedly block U.S. military access to their airspace, fearing Iranian retaliation.
February 6–27, 2026: Indirect nuclear talks in Geneva fail despite reported "significant progress".
February 28: Operation Roaring Lion begins. Joint U.S.-Israeli strikes hit a leadership meeting in Tehran, killing Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei and other top officials.
March 2, 2026: Hezbollah enters the war; Israel responds with a ground offensive into southern Lebanon.
March 3–15: Iran widens the conflict, striking U.S. and civilian targets across nine countries, including the UAE, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, and Bahrain.
March 16–22: Iran restricts traffic through the Strait of Hormuz, causing global oil prices to surge past $100 per barrel.
Today, March 23: Israel continues "wide-scale strikes" on infrastructure in Tehran as the war enters its fourth week.
